# Better Call Ryan — Demo Recording Script

Target length: about 1 minute 50 seconds. Record each voiceover block separately. The live-call section should use real product audio.

## Storyboard and voiceover

### 0:00–0:13 — The real-life problem

Visual: an illustrative shot of someone leaving a basketball court and noticing an incoming call. This may be AI-generated; add a small `Illustrative AI-generated scene` label.

> After seven years in the United States, I learned that classroom English and everyday English are not the same. Real conversations arrive without warning, especially phone calls.

### 0:13–0:27 — Why this product is different

Visual: transition from the illustrative scene to the real Better Call Ryan Home screen.

> Voice AI is already powerful. But I still have to open an app, choose a mode, invent a topic, and start the conversation. Better Call Ryan reverses that interaction. The practice calls you.

### 0:27–0:38 — Home and scheduling

Visual: show Alex, **Try a call now**, and **Schedule a call**. Briefly show the scheduling flow.

> This is Alex, a basketball friend. I can take a call now, or schedule a short practice call inside a safe time window.

### 0:38–0:48 — Incoming call

Visual: show the real incoming-call screen and answer it.

> When the call arrives, it feels like a phone call, not a chat box. The AI is already in character and begins with a real reason for calling.

### 0:48–1:08 — Real conversation

Visual and audio: use the actual call. Do not narrate over it. A simple response path is:

> Hey Alex. Yeah, I’m still around. What’s up?

> Kevin too? Okay, give me about twenty minutes. Where are we meeting?

> Sounds good. See you there. Have a good one.

It is fine if the AI says something different. Respond naturally; the point is to show a real unscripted exchange.

### 1:08–1:23 — Call Receipt

Visual: show the real post-call receipt and slowly move through its main cards.

> After we both say goodbye, the call ends and I receive a Call Receipt. It tells me whether I completed the task, what I understood, what I missed, and one more natural phrase I could use.

### 1:23–1:43 — Codex and GPT-5.6

Visual: show the README section **How Codex and GPT-5.6 contributed**, or keep the app visible and add simple text labels.

> I built Better Call Ryan with Codex. Codex helped turn the idea into the call state machine, scheduling flow, secure API boundary, and tested implementation. GPT Realtime powers the live conversation, while GPT-5.6 Sol handles structured scheduling and post-call feedback.

### 1:43–1:51 — Close

Visual: Better Call Ryan logo and product name.

> Better Call Ryan makes everyday English call you first.

## AI B-roll prompt

Use at most one short generated opening shot. Keep the actual product demo real.

```text
A six-second 16:9 cinematic documentary shot of an adult East Asian man walking away from an outdoor pickup basketball court at dusk. His phone gently vibrates in his hand and he glances down, slightly surprised by an incoming call. Natural city ambience, realistic human motion, subtle handheld camera, soft evening light, grounded everyday mood. No visible phone interface, no text, no logos, no trademarks, no famous person, no dramatic commercial styling.
```

Mute any generated audio and use the recorded voiceover. If video generation takes too long, generate one 16:9 still image and add a slow zoom in the editor.

## Fast editing checklist

- Canvas: 16:9, 1080p.
- Record the voiceover one block at a time; pauses between blocks will be removed in editing.
- Keep the real app and call audio for the core demo.
- Add English auto-captions, then manually correct `Codex`, `GPT Realtime`, and `GPT-5.6 Sol`.
- Do not use background music unless its submission rights are certain. Voiceover and call audio are enough.
- Export under three minutes and upload to YouTube as Public or Unlisted.
